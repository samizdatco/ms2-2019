What federal laws protect our medical information from improper exposure?

HIPAA/HITECH are two federal laws aimed at protecting the privacy and security of electronic health information. HITECH requires public notifications of breaches investigated by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' (HHS) Office for Civil Rights (OCR).

Who must follow HIPAA/HITECH?

  • Business Associate

    An entity or person that performs activities or services on ePHI on behalf of a covered entity.

  • Healthcare Provider

    Conduct certain business electronically (e.g. doctors, clinics, hospitals, psychologists, chiropractors, nursing homes, pharmacies, and dentists).

  • Health Plan

    Health insurance companies, HMOs, company health plans, and government programs that pay for health care, e.g. Medicare/Medicaid.

What is a Business Associate Agreement?

A provider enters into a BAA with an outside party when their outside party receives access to the provider's ePHI. A BAA "protects" the provider if the outside party breaches the ePHI.
