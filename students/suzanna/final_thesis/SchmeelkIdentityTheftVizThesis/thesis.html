What is HIPAA/HITECH?

HIPAA/HITECH are two federal laws aimed a protecting the privacy and security of electronic health information.

What is electronic Patient Health Information?

18 ePHI Identifiers

  • Names
  • All geographical subdivisions smaller than a State
  • All elements of dates (except year) for dates directly related to an (under 90) individual
  • Phone numbers
  • Fax numbers
  • Electronic mail addresses
  • Social Security numbers
  • Medical record numbers
  • Health plan beneficiary numbers
  • Account numbers
  • Certificate/license numbers
  • Vehicle identifiers and serial numbers
  • Device identifiers and serial numbers
  • Web Universal Resource Locators (URLs)
  • Internet Protocol (IP) address numbers
  • Biometric identifiers
  • Full photographic images
  • Any other unique identifying number, characteristic, or code

    Who is leaking ePHI data?

    Case study.

    The US Health and Human Services (HHS) Office of Civil Rights (OCR) investigates reported ePHI databreach cases. Anthem, a health plan, is still under investigation by the OCR for a 2017 data breach of 18,580 individuals.

    Who must follow HIPAA/HITECH?

    • Business Associate

      A person or entity that performs activities involving ePHI on behalf of, or provides services to, a covered entity.

    • Health Plan

      Health insurance companies, HMOs, company health plans, and certain government programs that pay for health care, such as Medicare and Medicaid.

    • Healthcare Provider

      Those that conduct certain business electronically—including most doctors, clinics, hospitals, psychologists, chiropractors, nursing homes, pharmacies, and dentists.

    Business Associate Agreements (BAA)

    A provider enters into a BAA with an outside party that receive access to Protected Health Information (PHI) which orignates with the provider. In the case of a data breach via the outside party of the providers ePHI, then both the provider and outside party may find themselves at fault with federal regulations.
