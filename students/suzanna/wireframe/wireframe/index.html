A Right to Private/Secure Medical Data: Demystifying the Data Breach

This study raises awareness on the data breach with a special focus on HIPAA breaches. Security-related breaches are happening at an unprecedented rate. In parallel, laws are being added and modified to adjust to help with the changing security landscape. This work helps to demystify and raise awareness about data breaches with a special focus on patient health information. Our awareness visualizations were created based on news articles, public government data, and interviews with institutional directors of Information Security.

On patient health data reported breaches, what were the causes of the breaches?

HIPAA covered entities which fall victim to data breaches, may be indicated on the OCR data breach portal. The OCR categorizes breaches into five main categories: Hacking/IT incident, Improper Disposal, Loss, Theft, and Unauthorized Access/Disclosure. The graph shows the proportion at which this standard incidents occurs.

On patient health data reported breaches, were proper business associate agreements in place?

HIPAA covered entities are required to put in place a Business Associates agreement when business associates obtain the protected data from covered entities. The Office of Civil Rights (OCR) maintains a portal of breaches under investigation. Their data shows how many breaches occurred by state with and without a proper Business Associates agreement.

Discussion of the results and their implications

Breaches occur with higher proportions in Hacking and IT incidents, as reported by the OCR.

Breaches occur across the country without proper Business Associate agreements in place, as reported by the OCR.
